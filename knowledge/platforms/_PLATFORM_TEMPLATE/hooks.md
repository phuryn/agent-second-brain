# [PLATFORM] Hooks

Hooks are opening and closing patterns that earn attention or lock in the ending. This file is a reference, not a rulebook. The goal is a library of patterns that have worked — not a formula to repeat.

Load this file when you're writing an opener or closer and want options, or when the current hook isn't landing.

## Opening Hooks

### Pattern: The Specific Failure
**Format:** State a specific, named failure — yours or someone else's — then pivot to the lesson.
**Signals that it's working:** Reader feels recognition, not inspiration-porn
**Signals it's failing:** Too much setup before the pivot; failure isn't specific enough

### Pattern: The Counterintuitive Claim
**Format:** State something that sounds wrong. Then immediately signal you'll prove it.
**Signals that it's working:** Reader wants to disagree, but reads on to find out why they're wrong
**Signals it's failing:** Claim is edgy but empty — no actual argument follows

### Pattern: The Precise Observation
**Format:** Describe something specific that many people have experienced but never named.
**Signals that it's working:** Reader thinks "yes, exactly" — feeling of being seen

## Closing Hooks

### Pattern: The Echo
**Format:** Return to the scene, image, or question from the opening — now seen differently.
**Signals that it's working:** Piece feels complete, not just finished
**Signals it's failing:** Echo feels forced; the opener and closer don't actually rhyme


### Pattern: The Upgraded Frame
**Format:** Restate the core insight, but at a higher level of abstraction than where you started.
**Signals that it's working:** Reader feels the idea has expanded
**Signals it's failing:** Abstraction is vague — sounds profound but says nothing new

### Pattern: The Actionable Ending
**Format:** Give the reader one concrete thing to do with what they just read.
**Signals that it's working:** Reader knows exactly what the next step is
**Signals it's failing:** Action feels tacked on; doesn't flow from the content

### Pattern: The Open Question
