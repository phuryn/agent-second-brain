# LinkedIn Rules

**Last updated:** 2026-04-01
**Confidence level:** high — based on 80+ posts over 6 months

---

## Hard Rules

These are non-negotiable. Violation = draft-gate failure.

| Rule | Reason | Source |
|------|--------|--------|
| No links in the body of the post | LinkedIn suppresses reach on posts with external links | Consistent across 12 link-vs-no-link tests; avg 40% reach drop with links |
| First line must work as a standalone sentence | LinkedIn shows only the first line before "see more" — if it doesn't earn the click, the post doesn't exist | Observed across all top-performing posts |
| No hashtags in the body — hashtags only at end, max 3 | Body hashtags interrupt reading flow; more than 3 hashtags is a signal of spam | Testing over 30 posts; top performers averaged 1-2 hashtags |
| Never start with "I" | Posts starting with "I" perform 25% below average on first-hour engagement | Observed across 20+ posts; mechanism unclear but consistent |

**Examples of hard-rule violations:**
- "Check out my latest article: [link]" — link in body, penalized
- "I spent 10 years learning this..." — starts with "I"
- Using 8 hashtags after a post — signals low quality to the algorithm

## Formatting Rules

**Length:**
- Short posts: 150-250 words (under 1,000 characters)
- Long-form: 600-1,200 words
- Sweet spot for long-form: 800-1,000 words — this is where comments/saves maximize

**Structure:**
- Paragraphs: 1-3 sentences max. LinkedIn is mobile-first; long paragraphs collapse visually.
- Use line breaks generously — white space improves skimmability
- Bullets: use for lists of 4+; never use bullets for fewer than 4 items (looks lazy)
- Numbered lists: use when order matters; bullets when it doesn't

**Formatting:**
- Bold: use sparingly — 2-3 key phrases per long-form post max
- Italics: avoid on LinkedIn (renders inconsistently on mobile)
- Emojis: 0-2 per post, only if they add meaning (not decoration)

## Benchmarks

Based on posts from Jan 2026 - Mar 2026, audience size ~12,000 followers.

| Metric | Baseline | Good | Great |
|--------|----------|------|-------|
| Impressions (long-form) | 3,000 | 8,000 | 20,000+ |
| Engagement rate | 2% | 4% | 7%+ |
| Comments | 8 | 20 | 50+ |
| Profile visits (24hr) | 30 | 80 | 200+ |

**Notes:**
- Benchmarks are for organic reach, no pod engagement
- Long-form performs 3x short-form on saves; short-form performs 2x long-form on comments
- Post time: Tuesday-Thursday, 7-9am EST performs best for this audience

## Platform-Specific Behaviors

- **Early engagement matters:** The first 60 minutes of engagement strongly influence total reach. Respond to every comment in the first hour.
- **Saves are the highest-value signal:** A post that gets saves but few comments still gets pushed. Saves = the algorithm reads it as valuable.
- **Native video is not worth it for this audience:** Product managers prefer text. Video posts average 50% below text-post engagement.
- **Reposts with commentary outperform empty reposts:** Always add 2-3 sentences when reposting someone else's content.

---

## What to Avoid

- Lists that are too long (10+ items) — reader abandons before the end
- "Humble brag" framing — "I'm honored to announce..." — performs poorly with skeptical PM audience
- Asking readers to share or like in the post body — feels desperate, no evidence it helps
- Passive voice in the opener — kills the energy

See `false-beliefs.md` for things that seem like they should hurt but don't.
