# Newsletter Templates

Templates for the three most-used issue formats.

---

## Template: NL-001 — The Deep Dive

**ID:** NL-001
**Name:** The Deep Dive
**When to use:** When you have one important topic with enough depth to sustain 800-1,200 words. The reader should feel like an expert on this topic after reading.
**Estimated length:** 800-1,100 words

**Structure:**
```
[Subject line: The specific insight or question — 30-45 chars]

[Opening: 2-4 sentences. The hook + why this matters now.]

[The setup: What most people do / believe about this topic — 1-2 paragraphs]

[The turn: What's actually true, or what they're missing — 2-3 paragraphs with mechanism]

[The detail: The most important specific thing — 1-2 paragraphs. This is where the depth lives.]

[The application: 3-5 concrete things to do with this — not vague advice]

[Close: One sentence that reframes the whole issue]

[P.S.: One link, one ask, or one additional insight]
```

**Example subject lines:**
- `Why roadmaps keep failing (it's not prioritization)` — 49 chars
- `The interview question that changes everything` — 46 chars
- `What OKRs are actually measuring` — 32 chars

**Notes:**
- The "turn" must have a mechanism — not just "what's true" but "why it's true and how it works"
- The application section is where most issues go vague — each item should be specific enough to do tomorrow
- P.S. is optional but high-value — use it when you have something that doesn't fit the body

## Template: NL-002 — The Curated Roundup

**ID:** NL-002
**Name:** The Curated Roundup
**When to use:** When you have 3-5 high-quality items worth the reader's attention, with your perspective on each. Not a link dump — each item needs a 2-4 sentence take.
**Estimated length:** 500-700 words

**Structure:**
```
[Subject line: What ties these items together — 30-45 chars]

[Opening: Why these items, why this week — 2-3 sentences]

[Item 1: Title / source]
[Your take: 2-4 sentences. What it is + why it matters + what you think about it]
[Link]

[Item 2]
[Take]
[Link]

[Item 3]
[Take]
[Link]

[Optional Item 4 or 5]

[Close: The thread that connects them — 1-2 sentences]

[P.S.: Optional]
```

**Notes:**
- Every item needs a real opinion — "this is interesting" is not a take
- Order items by how much you have to say about them, not by date
- Minimum 3 items; if you only have 2 worth including, use NL-001 or wait

## Template: NL-003 — The Case Study

**ID:** NL-003
**Name:** The Case Study
**When to use:** When you have a specific company, team, or situation that illustrates a broader principle. Works best when the case is recent (within 12 months) and specific.
**Estimated length:** 700-1,000 words

**Structure:**
```
[Subject line: The outcome or finding — not "a case study of X"]

[Opening: The result or moment that makes this case worth reading — start there, not at the beginning]

[Context: Who, what, when — 2-3 sentences. Enough to make it real without turning into a history lesson]

[What happened: The story with specific detail — 3-4 paragraphs]

[What we can learn: 3-4 explicit principles extracted from the case]

[How to apply: For each principle, what does this mean for the reader's situation]

[Close: What this case reveals about the broader topic]
```

**Notes:**
- Real company names are best (with permission). If not possible: "a 200-person B2B SaaS company" > "a company I worked with"
- The principles must be generalizable — not "Notion did X" but "teams that do X tend to get Y"
- Each principle needs to be specific enough to apply, not just observe
