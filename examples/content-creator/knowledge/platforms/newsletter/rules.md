# Newsletter Rules

**Last updated:** 2026-04-01
**Confidence level:** high — based on 24 issues sent, ~4,200 subscribers

---

## Hard Rules

| Rule | Reason | Source |
|------|--------|--------|
| One topic per issue | Multi-topic issues have 15% lower click-through on all links | A/B tested across 4 issues |
| Subject line: 30-45 characters | Open rate drops 8-12% above 55 characters on mobile | Consistent across 8 subject line tests |
| No more than 3 links in the body | Each additional link after the 3rd reduces click-through on all links (attention dilution) | Observed over 18 issues |
| Include a concrete takeaway by word 200 | Readers who reach word 200 without a clear "what's in it for me" do not continue | Inferred from scroll-depth data |

**Examples of violations:**
- Issue covering "roadmaps, discovery, AND stakeholder management" — three topics, weak on all three
- Subject line: "The complete guide to product strategy for B2B SaaS founders in 2026" (78 chars) — too long
- 7 links in a single issue — click-through on each drops to near zero

---

## Formatting Rules

**Length:**
- Minimum: 500 words (below this feels thin for this audience)
- Maximum: 1,500 words
- Sweet spot: 700-1,000 words

**Structure:**
- Opening hook: 2-4 sentences max before value starts delivering
- Subheadings: Use for issues over 600 words; readers scan before committing to read
- One pull quote: A bolded or set-apart line that works as the "quotable" of the issue
- Signature: Consistent sign-off (maintains voice continuity)

**Visual:**
- Plain text preferred over heavy HTML styling (renders consistently across email clients)
- One image max — and only if it adds information (not decoration)
- No full-width banners or heavy headers (slow load, feel like marketing)

## Benchmarks

Based on 24 issues, Jan-Mar 2026.

| Metric | Baseline | Good | Great |
|--------|----------|------|-------|
| Open rate | 38% | 45% | 55%+ |
| Click rate | 4% | 8% | 15%+ |
| Reply rate | 0.5% | 1.5% | 3%+ |
| Unsubscribes per issue | <0.3% | <0.2% | <0.1% |

**Notes:**
- Reply rate is the highest-quality signal — it requires the most reader effort
- Issues with a direct question to the reader at the end average 2x reply rate
- Best send window: Tuesday 8am EST (tested vs. Thursday and Monday — Tuesday wins by 4-6% open rate)

---

## Platform-Specific Behaviors

- **Replies train the inbox:** Readers who reply once are 4x more likely to open subsequent issues. Encourage replies early in the relationship.
- **The P.S. is high-value real estate:** Many readers skip to the P.S. Use it for one CTA or a secondary insight — not an afterthought.
- **Referral mechanics underperform with this audience:** Technical / senior audiences dislike gamification. The "forward to a friend" request works better than referral links.
- **Long-term consistency beats viral issues:** The highest-converting issues for signups are often not the top-performing issues for opens. Consistency builds trust.

---

## What to Avoid

- Issuing corrections in the same issue where the error appeared (makes the issue feel unfinished — send a separate correction)
- Teasing content that's "coming next week" — sets an expectation that's hard to always meet
- Ending with "if you enjoyed this, please share" — feels weak; a direct ask works better if you're going to ask
